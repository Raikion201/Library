“1984” is a dystopian novel by George Orwell set in a totalitarian society ruled by the Party led by Big Brother. The story follows Winston Smith, a member of the outer Party, who secretly rebels against the oppressive regime. In this society, the Party monitors and controls every aspect of life, manipulating truth and history through propaganda and surveillance. Winston starts a forbidden love affair with Julia, another Party member, and they join a resistance movement against the Party. However, they are eventually captured and tortured by the Thought Police, leading Winston to betray Julia. The novel explores themes of government surveillance, manipulation of truth, individual freedom, and the power of psychological manipulation. The ending is bleak, emphasizing the complete dominance of the Party and the destruction of Winston's rebellious spirit.
