“Becoming” is a memoir by Michelle Obama, the former First Lady of the United States. In this book, Obama chronicles her life, from her childhood on the South Side of Chicago to her years in the White House. She shares personal anecdotes about her family, upbringing, education, and early career. Obama reflects on the challenges she faced as an African American woman navigating various spheres, from her experiences at Princeton University and Harvard Law School to her professional life before entering public service. The memoir also delves into her relationship with Barack Obama, their courtship, marriage, and family life. It offers insights into the dynamics of their relationship, the sacrifices made during Barack Obama's political career, and the impact of their public roles on their family. Throughout the book, Obama addresses themes of identity, race, and the struggles and triumphs she encountered while balancing personal aspirations with the demands of public life. She also discusses her initiatives as First Lady, focusing on issues like education, health, and empowerment. "Becoming" provides a personal and introspective look at Michelle Obama's journey, offering inspiration and insights into her experiences as a woman, a mother, and a public figure.
