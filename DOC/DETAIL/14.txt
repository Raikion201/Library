“Dracula” by Bram Stoker is a classic Gothic horror novel that follows the story of Count Dracula, a centuries-old vampire from Transylvania, and his attempt to move from his castle to England to spread the undead curse. The novel is structured as a series of journals, letters, newspaper clippings, and diary entries, narrated by various characters. It begins with Jonathan Harker, a young lawyer, traveling to Dracula's castle to assist with a real estate transaction. Harker soon realizes that he is a prisoner in the castle, and he discovers Dracula's vampiric nature. Meanwhile, back in England, strange occurrences and unexplained illnesses start plaguing Lucy Westenra, a friend of Harker's fiancée, Mina Murray. As Lucy's health deteriorates, her friends seek the help of Professor Abraham Van Helsing, an expert in obscure diseases. They eventually realize that Lucy has fallen victim to Dracula's nocturnal visits, turning her into one of the undead. As the novel progresses, Dracula makes his way to England, terrorizing the group and threatening Mina. Van Helsing and a group of allies work to hunt down and defeat Dracula, using various methods and tools, including garlic, crucifixes, and wooden stakes. The story culminates in a thrilling chase across Europe as they pursue Dracula back to Transylvania, ultimately leading to a final confrontation at his castle. "Dracula" explores themes of Victorian anxieties, superstition, sexuality, and the clash between modernity and ancient evil. It remains a quintessential work in vampire literature and has had a profound influence on Gothic fiction and popular culture.
